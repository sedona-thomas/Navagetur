How does this widget work?

This widget takes in a password and returns an estimate of how long it would take to brute force crack this password on the current device.

Warning

If you are using this application on a website, do not enter your real passwords. Instead, go to the github repository and download the application to use on your personal device. When run locally, all processing of passwords is done on device and passwords are never stored.

Why is this widget important?

Social media and other forms have allowed users to share more of themselves online but this should be carefully approached as you do not want to give too much of your information away, leaving you susceptible to online attacks. A complex password makes it prohibitively difficult for common hackers to break into a machine and will force them to look for other targets thus you must ensure your passwords are strong. The more difficult the password, the lower the likelihood that one's computer will fall victim to unwanted intrusions. Passwords must be long and complex. Passwords should contain at least ten characters and have a combination of characters such as commas, percent signs, and parentheses, as well as upper-case and lower-case letters and numbers.

What do you learn from this widget?

The goal is to learn just how easy it truly is to crack your password. This tool is to empower you to create better passwords. We also want you to learn how to share information on the internet without compromising your own safety. We want you to learn that Strong passwords shouldn’t include references to personal information such as names, birthdays, addresses, or phone numbers. Secure passwords include a variety of random characters, numbers, and letters to make the password more complex. Reusing the same password for different accounts puts you at risk of credential stuffing attacks frequently used by cybercriminals. Lastly, Hackers use malicious programs that can process every word found in a dictionary to crack passwords. Stay away from using proper nouns and other standalone dictionary words that could lead to an unsecure password.

Key Terms

  • Encryption: Encryption is a means of securing digital data using one or more mathematical techniques, along with a password or "key" used to decrypt the information
  • Decrypt: A text that has been decoded
  • Cryptography: Cryptography is a method of protecting information and communications through the use of codes, so that only those for whom the information is intended can read and process it
  • Cybercriminals vs Hackers: ​​Cybercriminals hack and infiltrate computer systems with malicious intent, while hackers only seek to find new and innovative ways to use a system, be it for good or bad
